# Limitations

- The package guides research; it does not create browsing, code execution, private-data access, or paid databases.
- Source availability, definitions, geographic coverage, and recency vary by market.
- Interviews, surveys, clicks, and waitlists are vulnerable to selection and hypothetical bias.
- Scripts verify arithmetic and selected guardrails, not the truth of inputs.
- Scenario labels are not probabilities. The package does not fit a statistical forecast unless suitable data and methods are supplied.
- E0-E4 labels describe evidence stages by dimension; they are not success probabilities or universal certification levels.
- Default 2×, 5×, 10×, and 20× baseline alerts are diagnostics that require context and can be inappropriate for zero, constrained, or structurally changed baselines.
- Capacity, cash, regulations, taxes, insurance, engineering, and implementation can change after a report's cutoff date.
- Market research cannot eliminate entrepreneurial uncertainty or guarantee that a pilot will scale.
- The package is not legal, accounting, tax, investment, lending, medical, engineering, insurance, or regulatory advice.
- Hosts and models may interpret instructions differently; deterministic tests do not replace behavior Evals and human review for material decisions.
- External links can move or become unavailable. Verify source dates and locators at report time.
