## Method in one page

1. Detect browsing, computation, files, and internal-data capabilities.
2. Define a concrete decision, deadline, commitment, user loss constraint, success threshold, failure threshold, and stop condition.
3. Route lifecycle and business model; choose research plan, screening memo, or full report.
4. Build claim, assumption, evidence, gap, search, dependency, and calculation records.
5. Prefer verified internal behavior for operating businesses and paid tests for pre-launch businesses.
6. Evaluate evidence authority and fit separately at claim level; never average a composite score.
7. Use conditional funnels, coherent units, capacity ceilings, and matched platform supply and demand.
8. Compare alternatives and the counterfactual.
9. Stress correlated assumptions, cash, capacity, implementation, and regulation.
10. Rate maturity by material dimension and let the weakest critical dimension bind.
11. Recommend only against declared thresholds; otherwise state evidence insufficiency.
12. End with the next gate and lowest-cost validation.

The methodology adapts public small-business market-research, feasibility-study, option-appraisal, and data-quality principles. Those frameworks inform the process; their sector-specific numbers and public-sector objectives are not transferred automatically to an individual business.
