# Research Workflow

## Contents

1. Capability gate
2. Decision contract
3. Routing
4. Claim-driven research
5. Stopping rules
6. Output depth

## 1. Run the capability gate

Record four capabilities as `available`, `unavailable`, or `unknown`:

| Capability | If available | If unavailable |
|---|---|---|
| Current web research | Verify current external claims | Produce queries and a source-request list |
| Local computation | Run bundled scripts | Show formulas without fabricated results |
| User files | Audit supplied evidence | List the minimum files needed |
| Internal operating data | Establish a verified baseline | State that operating conclusions are limited |

Do not imply that installing a Skill adds browsing, paid data, or code execution to a host that lacks those capabilities.

## 2. Define the decision contract

Record before broad research:

- decision and decision owner;
- decision deadline;
- geography and service radius;
- offer, customer, channel, and business model;
- lifecycle: pre-launch, operating, expansion, platform, or event-driven;
- proposed cash, time, contract, inventory, and staffing commitments;
- reversible and irreversible portions;
- user-supplied maximum loss or at-risk budget;
- success, failure, and stop thresholds;
- time horizon and data cutoff;
- excluded questions.

If the user cannot supply a loss constraint, do not output a numeric investment ceiling. Use a validation sequence instead.

## 3. Route the work

### Pre-launch

Use local demographics, trade area, competitors, supplier quotes, costs, regulations, reference classes, interviews, deposits, preorders, pop-ups, and pilots. Label every external operating number as a proxy or comparable.

### Operating business

Prioritize transactions, refunds, inquiries, footfall, conversion, order value, contribution, capacity, stockouts, acquisition, and seasonality. Check data completeness, definitions, returns, cancellations, and selection bias before treating internal records as ground truth.

### Expansion

Separate the proven unit model from transfer assumptions. Test new-location differences, cannibalization, management load, fixed-cost step changes, ramp time, and a staged rollout.

### Platform

Model supply, demand, and matching independently. Require evidence for onboarding, availability, geographic and temporal liquidity, trust, insurance, disputes, disintermediation, and subsidy needs.

### Event-driven decision

Estimate incremental rather than total attendance. Establish the counterfactual, route and time overlap, purchasing occasion, exposure, conversion, displacement, closures, transport constraints, and capacity.

## 4. Research claims, not topics

For each material claim, create a record containing:

1. claim and decision consequence;
2. metric, unit, geography, population, and period;
3. support condition and refutation condition;
4. best available direct evidence;
5. alternative explanation;
6. search queries, languages, and official classification terms;
7. accepted and rejected sources with reasons;
8. remaining uncertainty and next evidence action.

Search in this order when relevant:

1. verified internal transactions and operations;
2. local official and administrative sources;
3. regional and national statistics;
4. regulators, transport operators, tourism bodies, and event organizers;
5. transparent research and regulatory filings;
6. competitors, suppliers, property listings, and customer reviews as market signals;
7. news and other discovery sources that lead to originals.

Search for contrary evidence: closures, low utilization, complaints, regulation, insurance exclusions, substitutes, cancellations, seasonality, historical event outcomes, and failed comparables.

## 5. Stop research deliberately

Stop and move to validation when any condition holds:

- new secondary sources no longer change the decision;
- the required evidence gate has been met;
- another source would repeat an existing dependency cluster;
- only a pilot or transaction can resolve the material uncertainty;
- the cost of information exceeds its likely decision value;
- the data is legally, commercially, or technically unavailable.

Return `evidence insufficient` when a material unknown can reverse the decision and no safe evidence path has yet been completed.

## 6. Choose output depth

### Research plan

Use at E0 or when the decision contract is incomplete. Deliver claims, sources, missing inputs, experiments, thresholds, budget, and stop rules.

### Screening memo

Use for a narrow or reversible decision. Include decision, evidence profile, key economics or threshold analysis, options, material risks, and next gate.

### Full feasibility report

Use for a material commitment after sufficient evidence is available. Load the report template and include only applicable modules. Do not force TAM/SAM/SOM, technical analysis, or public-economic appraisal into cases where they do not affect the decision.
