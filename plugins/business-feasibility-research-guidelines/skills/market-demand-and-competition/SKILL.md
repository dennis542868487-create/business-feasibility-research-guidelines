# Market Demand and Competition

Estimate reachable, qualified demand rather than quoting a large market total.

## Procedure

1. Define the decision, offer, customer, purchasing occasion, geography, period, channel, and capacity.
2. Separate operating businesses from pre-launch ideas. Use verified transactions first for an operating business; use comparables, proxies, deposits, pilots, and experiments for pre-launch work.
3. Define claims and refutation conditions before searching.
4. Map direct competitors, indirect alternatives, self-service, delay, and non-purchase.
5. Normalize price, package, capacity, availability, customer segment, geography, and period before comparison.
6. Build a conditional funnel. Ensure every rate is conditional on the preceding stage and flag overlapping stages.
7. Use at least one independent reference class or direct behavior source when credible. Do not count two models using the same input as triangulation.
8. Cap fulfilled orders with capacity. For platforms, calculate supply and demand separately and match them with `min(matched_supply, matched_demand)`.
9. Compare estimates only after reconciling definitions. Preserve a wide range or return insufficient evidence when they remain incompatible.
10. Design the lowest-cost paid-behavior test for the binding uncertainty.

For a two-sided platform, label the supply and demand sections explicitly. At E0-E1, include a manual or concierge pilot that tests both sides in the same local time window, with insurance and regulatory clearance as stop gates when applicable.

Read [the business-model formulas](../business-feasibility-research-guidelines/references/business-models.md) and [the evidence standard](../business-feasibility-research-guidelines/references/evidence-standard.md). Use the bundled math engine for material funnels when available.

## Never infer directly

- population to customers;
- attendees or tourists to store orders;
- website traffic to paid revenue;
- ratings to profitability;
- competitor presence to unmet demand;
- waitlist or interview intent to paid conversion;
- global TAM to a local or early-stage central forecast.

Return demand evidence, counterevidence, funnel inputs, reachable range, capacity cap, substitutes, dependency map, and validation thresholds.
